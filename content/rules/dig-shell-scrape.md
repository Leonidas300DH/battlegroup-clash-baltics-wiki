# Dig Shell Scrape Action (4.8)

Only a **Foot [[force-elements|Force Element (FE)]]** may take this Action. It creates a temporary defensive earthwork providing negative [[glossary|Dice Roll Modifiers (DRMs)]] against incoming fire and [[assault|Assaults]].

> A shell scrape is a hastily dug defensive position.

## Mechanics

- The FE **remains in place** and receives a **Digging marker** (placed under the bottom edge of the counter)
- A second Dig Shell Scrape Action **flips** the Digging marker to its **Shell Scrape** side
- Can be taken in **any Terrain type**
- The marker is **removed** if the FE Moves or Repositions

## DRM Protection

| Marker | [[glossary|DRM]] against Direct Fire | [[glossary|DRM]] against Indirect Fire | [[glossary|DRM]] against Assault |
|--------|------------------------|--------------------------|---------------------|
| **Digging** | -1 | -1 | -1 |
| **Shell Scrape** | -2 | -2 | -2 |

## Important Notes

- Other Actions can be taken **between** placing the Digging marker and flipping it to Shell Scrape — it does not require consecutive Actions
- An FE can take [[direct-fire|Direct Fire]], [[hold|Hold]], or [[overwatch|Overwatch]] and the marker **stays in place**
- The marker is only removed on **Move** or **Reposition**
- Shell Scrape markers **may begin the game in play** (set by the scenario)
- In Assaults: Shell Scrape markers are **removed** if the Defenders lose (green-shaded result), but **remain** if Defenders win (unless the FE Repositions)

## Tactical Value

Shell Scrapes are extremely powerful for static infantry defense:
- Combined with Cover: a Foot FE in Dense Wood with Shell Scrape has **-4 DRM** (Cover -2, Shell Scrape -2)
- From the example of play: the Russian player decided against using his FPV against a NATO FE with Dense Wood Cover + Shell Scrape because the -4 DRM meant needing to roll 11+ on 2D6
