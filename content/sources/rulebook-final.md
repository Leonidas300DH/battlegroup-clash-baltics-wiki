# Rulebook - FINAL

**File:** `raw/Rulebook - FINAL.pdf`
**Pages:** 32
**Type:** Core rulebook
**Author:** James Buckley (Sapper Studio)
**System designer:** Ivor Gardiner (Vedette Consulting)

## Key Takeaways

- BC:B is a **company-level** tactical wargame simulating NATO vs Russia combat in **Estonia**
- Based on the British Army's professional wargame **Battlegroup Wargame System (BGWS)**
- Unique among commercial wargames for requiring pre-game **planning via a Sync Matrix**
- Uses bespoke maps based on Estonian satellite imagery — no hex grid, free-flowing movement
- Three play modes: [[game-modes|Basic, Standard, Advanced]] — each builds on the previous
- 2-6 players, 5-6 turns per scenario, each turn = 15-30 minutes of game time
- Designed as both a hobby game and a **professional military education (PME)** tool
- Licensed under **Creative Commons BY-NC-SA**

## Detailed Summary

### Overview & Scope
The game covers NATO vs Russia engagements in the Baltic states (specifically Estonia). Unit scale is platoon/section level; the largest scenario pits a NATO Battlegroup against a Russian Motorised Rifle Battalion. Each 1cm on the map = 100m. Grid Squares = 1,000m x 1,000m.

### Components
- **[[force-elements|Force Elements (FEs)]]**: military units on the map (Foot, Wheeled, Tracked)
- **[[support-assets|Support Assets (SAs)]]**: off-map artillery, [[support-assets|First Person View drone (FPV)]] drones, on-map [[support-assets|Unmanned Aerial Support drone (UAS)]]
- **[[task-groups|Task Groups]]**: organizational groupings of [[force-elements|FEs]]
- **[[stacking|Stacks]]**: combined [[force-elements|FEs]] (Tracked [[force-elements|Platoon (PL)]] + Mounted Foot [[force-elements|PL]] and/or Embedded [[force-elements|Section (SECT)]])
- **[[atgm|ATGMs]]**: anti-tank weapons (NLAW, Javelin for NATO; RPG, Stabber for Russia)
- **[[electronic-warfare|Electronic Transmission (ETX)]] markers, [[electronic-warfare|Electronic Warfare (EW)]] Chits, Target markers**: electronic warfare system
- **Information markers**: Reorg, Overwatch/Activated, Moved/Activated, Suppressed/Disrupted, Digging/Shell Scrape, Smoke, Fire Mission
- **Maps**: 4 maps (A-D), based on Estonian terrain, 1cm = 100m
- **Dice**: 2x D6 (red, blue) + 1x green D6 (turn tracker)

### Game Structure
1. **[[preparation-phase|Preparation Phase]]** (once): read scenario, set up, write [[sync-matrix|Sync Matrix]]
2. **[[command-phase|Command Phase]]** (each turn): [[morale|Rally]], [[orders|New Orders]], [[electronic-warfare|EW Activity]]
3. **[[execution-phase|Execution Phase]]** (each turn): [[initiative|Determine Initiative]], [[execution-phase|Action Round]], Clean-up

### Actions (9 total)
1. [[move|Move]] — move up to Movement Allowance
2. [[move-fire|Move-Fire]] — move and fire in same action
3. [[direct-fire|Direct Fire]] — shoot at visible enemy
4. [[indirect-fire|Indirect Fire]] — mortar/artillery/FPV strikes
5. [[assault|Assault]] — close-quarters combat
6. [[hold|Hold]] — stay in place, reposition up to 250m
7. [[overwatch|Overwatch]] — hold + ability to Reactive Fire
8. [[dig-shell-scrape|Dig Shell Scrape]] — create defensive earthwork (Foot only)
9. [[replendem|REPLENDEM]] — resupply ammo (Advanced mode, A1 Echelon only)

### Key Mechanics
- **[[line-of-sight|Line of Sight (LoS)]]**: max 3,000m (4,000m for Recce), blocked by Dense Woods/Urban/elevation/[[force-elements|FEs]]
- **[[terrain|Terrain]]**: Open, Light Wood, Dense Wood, Light Urban, Urban, Hills, Rivers, Roads
- **[[combat-effectiveness|Hits & Resilience]]**: [[force-elements|FEs]] have Resilience (1-4); each hit reduces by 1; at 0 = Eliminated
- **[[direct-fire|Firepower]]**: Apers (green) vs Atk (purple/gold) — color-coded targeting system
- **[[morale|Morale]]**: Good → Suppressed → Disrupted → Broken (= Eliminated)
- **[[electronic-warfare|Electronic Warfare (EW)]]**: actions generate [[electronic-warfare|Electronic Transmission (ETX)]] markers; these become Target markers and [[electronic-warfare|EW]] Chits

### Factions
- **[[nato|NATO]]**: higher [[force-elements|Troop Quality (TQ)]], more Resilience, Mission Command flexibility, lower Move-Fire penalty
- **[[russia|Russia]]**: more [[support-assets|FPV]]/artillery fire missions, [[electronic-warfare|EW]] advantage (draws 2 [[electronic-warfare|EW]] Chits keeps 1 in Advanced), but rigid doctrine (cannot deviate from Orders in Advanced mode)

### Design Philosophy
- Counters are **approximations** of position, not exact locations
- No concealment mechanics — drones see everything
- No air/aviation — contested airspace assumed
- Encourages house-ruling and experimentation
- Scarcity is intentional — forces are below strength, ammo is limited

### Optional Rules
- [[optional-rules|Weather]] (Low clouds/Storms affecting [[support-assets|FPV]] and [[support-assets|UAS]])
- [[optional-rules|Flexible Task Groups]] (custom [[task-groups|Task Group (TGp)]] composition)
- [[optional-rules|More Smoke]] (extended smoke coverage)
- [[optional-rules|LoS through Partial Obstacles]] (250m limit)

## Notable Quotes

> "BC:B is intended to serve as a tactical-level land warfare learning and training tool that will appeal both to professional and hobby gamers looking to better understand combat on the modern battlefield."

> "Players are recommended against applying a rigid approach to movement, positioning and line of sight. A counter is intended to represent an 'approximation' of the FE's position."

> "In the Scenarios represented you have the cream of European NATO fighting against poorer trained, motivated and equipped Russian forces. Hence the NATO forces have greater resilience and troop quality. However, the Russians have greater numbers and are better at drone and electronic warfare."
