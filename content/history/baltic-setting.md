# The Baltic Setting

BC:B is set in the **Baltic states** — specifically **Estonia** — reflecting the region's status as a potential flashpoint between [[nato|NATO]] and [[russia|Russia]].

## Why Estonia?

- A British Army Battlegroup is **permanently stationed** in Estonia (NATO's Enhanced Forward Presence)
- Estonia, Latvia, and Lithuania share borders with Russia (and Kaliningrad for Lithuania/Poland)
- The region has been identified as a likely scenario for a potential NATO-Russia military confrontation
- Estonia's diverse terrain (forests, wetlands, urban areas, rivers) provides varied tactical challenges

## Scenario Assumptions

The game's scenarios assume:

1. **Near-future conflict** — a hypothetical Russian military action in the Baltics
2. **Conventional warfare** — ground combat between mechanized/armored forces
3. **European NATO forces** — not US-centric; represents the coalition of European NATO nations
4. **Russian forces post-attrition** — Russian [[force-elements|Platoons (PLs)]] have Resilience 3 (vs NATO's 4), reflecting lower troop numbers and the attritional effects of prior operations
5. **Contested airspace** — neither side has air superiority, so no fixed-wing or helicopter aviation is featured
6. **Drone-saturated battlefield** — [[support-assets|First Person View drone (FPV)]] and [[support-assets|Unmanned Aerial Support drone (UAS)]] drones provide near-total surveillance; no concealment mechanics
7. **Scarcity** — both sides operate with limited resources, below-strength formations, and constrained ammunition

## Maps

The four game maps (A through D) are based on **actual terrain and features from Estonia**, created from satellite imagery by map artist Shayne Logan. Each map represents real Estonian geography with:
- Forests (Light Wood, Dense Wood)
- Urban areas (Light Urban, Urban)
- Rivers and bridges
- Road networks (Major, Minor)
- Hills and elevation
- Open terrain

## Historical Context

The game draws on real-world military planning and doctrine:
- **NATO Enhanced Forward Presence**: multinational battlegroups deployed in the Baltic states since 2017
- **Russian doctrine**: centralized planning, doctrinal rigidity, emphasis on fires and electronic warfare
- **Lessons from recent conflicts**: the importance of drones, [[electronic-warfare|Electronic Warfare (EW)]], and combined arms
- **Force design**: task-organized battalion battlegroups typical of NATO's approach to expeditionary warfare

| Aspect | BC:B Representation |
