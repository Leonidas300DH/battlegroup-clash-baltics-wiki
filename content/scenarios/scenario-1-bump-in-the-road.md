# Scenario 1 — A Bump In The Road

The introductory scenario for BC:B. A Russian mechanised force pushes southwest to seize the bridge at Sindi before NATO engineers can demolish it. NATO fights a delaying action with under-strength forces.

## Overview

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Map | **Map C** |
| Turns | **5** |
| Initiative (Turn 1) | [[russia\|Russia]] |
| Bridge location | **E220/N234** |
| Town | **Sindi** (GS E21/N22) |
| Model Sync Matrix | Provided for both sides |

## Scenario-Specific Rules

- Only **one** friendly Tracked [[force-elements|Platoon (PL)]]-sized [[force-elements|Force Element (FE)]] may cross any Bridge on the map per Turn (no limit on [[force-elements|Sections (SECTs)]])
- NATO: no specific action needed to blow the bridge — it is assumed to happen automatically if NATO meets the Primary Objective
- NATO forces are placed **after** both sides draft their [[sync-matrix|Sync Matrices]]

## Situation

[[russia|Russia]] is on the offensive, pushing southwest to cut off NATO's northern force. The bridge north of Sindi is critical for Russian lines of communication. Intelligence says NATO has about half the Russian force strength defending the bridge, and hasn't yet rigged it for demolition.

[[nato|NATO]] is fighting a delaying action. Forces are stretched thin and already attrited (all [[force-elements|Platoons (PLs)]] start at Resilience 3). No artillery support — only a Mortar platoon and [[support-assets|First Person View drone (FPV)]] drone. The mission is to hold until engineers can blow the bridge.

## Strategic Analysis

### NATO Strategy (from model Sync Matrix)
- **Phase 1 (T1-2)**: Disrupt Russian advance with Overwatch fire from prepared positions. Use Javelin (1-B-1) as the primary anti-armour weapon
- **Phase 2 (T3)**: Begin withdrawing [[task-groups|TGp]] 3 north of the bridge. [[task-groups|TGp]] 1 continues disrupting
- **Phase 3 (T4-5)**: Concentrate remaining forces at the bridge. Hold until end of Turn 5
- **Fire Plan**: Mortar and [[support-assets|FPV]] target likely Russian approach routes (GI E25/N22, then shift west)

### Russian Strategy (from model Sync Matrix)
- **Phase 1 (T1-2)**: Three-axis advance: [[task-groups|TGp]] 1 from north, [[task-groups|TGp]] 2 from center, [[task-groups|TGp]] 3 from south. Recce screens ahead
- **Phase 2 (T3-4)**: [[task-groups|TGp]] 2 and 3 attack NATO positions south of river, clear the way to bridge
- **Phase 3 (T5)**: [[task-groups|TGp]] 1 and 2 converge on bridge from north. [[task-groups|TGp]] 3 secures Sindi if that's the secondary objective
- **Fire Plan**: Concentrate 152mm on GI E22/N23 (bridge approaches). [[support-assets|FPV]] shifts from E23/N22 to E22/N23

### Key Terrain
- **The Bridge (E220/N234)**: the decisive point — whoever controls it at end of Turn 5 determines the Primary Objective
- **Sindi (GS E21/N22)**: the Secondary Objective for both sides depending on the roll
- **Dense Woods** (east of river): provide Cover for NATO Foot [[force-elements|FEs]] with Shell Scrapes but block vehicle movement
- **Open terrain** (east approach): exposes Russian Stacks to NATO Reactive Fire during approach
