# NATO

NATO represents a generic **European NATO force** in BC:B — not any single nation's army. The force structure is based on British/European NATO battalion battlegroup organization, adapted for the game.

## Faction Characteristics

| Attribute             | Value                                   | Notes                              |
| --------------------- | --------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Counter color         | **Grey**                                |                                    |
| Typical [[force-elements|Platoon (PL)]] Resilience | **4**                                   | Higher than Russia (3)             |
| Typical [[force-elements|Troop Quality (TQ)]]            | **Higher**                              | Better trained, more cohesive      |
| Move-Fire penalty     | **-1 [[glossary|Dice Roll Modifier (DRM)]]**                              | Better fire-on-the-move capability |
| Artillery             | **155mm**                               |                                    |
| [[atgm|Anti-Tank Guided Missiles (ATGMs)]]                 | **NLAW** (1,000m), **Javelin** (3,000m) | Blue font on markers               |

## Doctrinal Advantage: Mission Command (Advanced Mode)

In the [[command-phase|Command Phase]] New Orders step, NATO may issue **New Orders to a single Task Group** without:
- Placing Reorg markers on [[force-elements|Force Elements (FEs)]]
- Generating [[electronic-warfare|Electronic Transmission (ETX)]] for the opponent

This means the Task Group can **act immediately** on the New Orders, rather than losing a turn to reorganization.

> [!note] Design note
> NATO doctrine seeks to empower decision-making on the ground. Mission Command abstractly represents the flexibility and initiative that NATO commanders exercise at the tactical level.

## Strengths

- **Higher quality troops**: better [[force-elements|TQ]] means better [[glossary|DRMs]] on fires and assaults
- **More resilient**: [[force-elements|PL]] starting Resilience of 4 vs Russia's 3
- **[[mission-command|Mission Command]]**: one free Order change per turn (Advanced mode)
- **Better Move-Fire**: only -1 [[glossary|DRM]] vs Russia's -2
- **Javelin [[atgm|ATGMs]]**: 3,000m range, effective against [[force-elements|Main Battle Tanks (MBTs)]]

## Weaknesses

- **Fewer numbers**: typically outnumbered by Russian forces
- **Fewer Fire Missions**: less artillery and [[support-assets|First Person View drone (FPV)]] ammunition
- **[[electronic-warfare|Electronic Warfare (EW)]] disadvantage**: only draws 1 [[electronic-warfare|EW]] Chit (Russia draws 2, keeps 1 in Advanced)
- **Scarcity**: below-strength platoons, limited supplies

## Equipment Notes

- Forces represent the "cream of European NATO"
- Equipment adapted from BGWS British Army specifics to generic NATO standard
- 120mm Mortars (common across NATO, replacing BGWS's British 81mm)
- NLAW is a short-range (1,000m) anti-tank weapon
- Javelin is a long-range (3,000m) anti-tank guided missile

## Playing as NATO

From the [[rulebook-final|example of play]] and design notes:
- Use **Overwatch** aggressively — your better [[force-elements|TQ]] and Javelins make Reactive Fire devastating
- Leverage **Cover and Shell Scrapes** — your infantry's higher Resilience combined with defensive [[glossary|DRMs]] makes them very hard to dislodge
- **Mission Command** allows adapting to surprises without wasting a turn
- Quality over quantity — each [[force-elements|FE]] matters more, so protect them
