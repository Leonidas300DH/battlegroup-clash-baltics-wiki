# Russia

In BC:B, Russia represents the **Russian armed forces** operating in the Baltic states in a hypothetical near-future invasion of Estonia. Russian forces field mechanised infantry, main battle tanks, reconnaissance, artillery, and drones — typically organised around Motorised Rifle structures at battalion level (the largest scenario pits a Russian Motorised Rifle Battalion against a NATO Battlegroup).

Compared to [[nato|NATO]], Russian units are **more numerous** but individually **less well trained, motivated, and equipped**. They compensate with **superior numbers, more artillery and drone fire missions, and stronger electronic warfare capabilities**. Their lower [[force-elements|Troop Quality (TQ)]] and [[combat-effectiveness|Resilience]] reflect both smaller unit sizes within similar formations and the attritional effects of the ongoing invasion being simulated.

## Doctrinal Constraint: Red Doctrine (Advanced Mode)

In Advanced mode, Russia operates under strict doctrinal rigidity:

- **Cannot deviate** from pre-game [[orders|Orders]] or [[fire-plans|Fire Plans]] — ever
- **Cannot issue [[orders#New Orders|New Orders]]**
- Cannot change Fire Plans
- Cannot target enemies with Indirect Fire outside the Fire Plan area
- The "Adherence to Orders" and "Moving to Safety" rules **do not apply** (Russian Task Groups must always follow their Orders)
- If a Russian Task Group cannot fulfil an Order due to delay, it should attempt it on following turns until a new pre-written Order applies

> [!warning]
> This means Russian planning must be **exceptionally thorough** during the Preparation Phase. Mistakes in the Sync Matrix cannot be corrected mid-game.

## Compensating Advantages

To offset doctrinal rigidity, Russia receives:

| Advantage | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **More Fire Missions** | Additional artillery (152mm) and [[support-assets|First Person View drone (FPV)]] Fire Missions in Advanced mode [[glossary|Order of Battle (ORBAT)]]s |
| **[[electronic-warfare|Electronic Warfare (EW)]] superiority** | Draws **2 [[electronic-warfare|EW]] Chits**, keeps 1 (before NATO draws) in Advanced mode |
| **More forces** | Greater numbers of [[force-elements|Force Elements (FEs)]] in typical scenarios |

## Strengths

- **Numerical superiority**: more [[force-elements|FEs]] on the map
- **More firepower**: additional Fire Missions for artillery and [[support-assets|FPV]]
- **[[electronic-warfare|EW]] advantage**: draws 2 [[electronic-warfare|EW]] Chits and picks the better one
- **Stabber [[atgm|ATGMs]]**: effective long-range anti-tank (Stabber can only be taken by Tracked [[force-elements|FEs]])
- **[[support-assets|FPV]] expertise**: more drone fire missions available

## Weaknesses

- **Lower troop quality**: worse [[glossary|DRMs]] on fires, assaults, and Rally tests
- **Less resilient**: [[force-elements|PLs]] start at 3 Resilience (Eliminated after 3 Hits, not 4)
- **[[red-doctrine|Red Doctrine]]**: zero flexibility in Advanced mode — stuck with pre-game plan
- **Worse Move-Fire**: -2 [[glossary|DRM]] penalty (double NATO's penalty)
- **RPGs**: shorter range than NATO's [[atgm|ATGMs]]

## Equipment Notes

- Stabber is a catch-all name for various Russian [[atgm|ATGMs]]
- RPG is classified as an [[atgm|ATGM]] in BC:B (loosely defined term)
- Stabbers can **only** be taken by Russian Tracked [[force-elements|FEs]]
- Lower Resilience reflects both lower troop numbers in similar-sized units and attrition from prior operations

## Playing as Russia

From the [[rulebook-final|design notes]] and example of play:
- **Plan meticulously** — your Sync Matrix is your only chance to set the plan; no changes allowed
- **Use artillery aggressively** — you have more Fire Missions; soften up positions before advancing
- **Leverage numbers** — you can afford to lose more [[force-elements|FEs]] than NATO can
- **Concentrate fire** — your [[support-assets|FPV]] Double-Tap is valuable for eliminating priority targets
- **Exploit [[electronic-warfare|EW]]** — your 2-for-1 [[electronic-warfare|EW]] Chit draw can deliver powerful effects
- **Accept rigidity** — don't fight the Red Doctrine; write Orders that account for contingencies
