# Orders (Standard/Advanced Mode)

Orders are short instructions based on **verbs from the Orders Table** (Player Aid 6), taken from the British Army UK Staff Officers' Handbook. An Order applies to a [[task-groups|Task Group]] for a Turn.

## Using Orders

Any Action taken by a [[force-elements|Force Element (FE)]] **must correspond** with its Task Group's current Order. The controlling side determines which Action is most appropriate — there is flexibility in interpretation.

> Example: "Seize the Airport" allows [[force-elements|FEs]] to Move, Fire, Assault — anything that furthers the objective. Some [[force-elements|FEs]] might advance while others provide covering fire. The Order doesn't specify who does what.

## Adherence to Orders

An Order will be in one of three states:

| State | Response |
|-------|----------|
| **Achievable** | Carry it out (most of the time) |
| **Not achievable** | Issue [[#New Orders]] (e.g., too many losses, too delayed) |
| **Not appropriate** | Issue New Orders (e.g., battlefield situation has changed) |

> [!note]
> There is **no mechanical penalty** for not following an Order. The game assumes players are playing to learn and will follow orders in good faith.

## Moving to Safety

If following the Order would leave [[force-elements|FEs]] in extreme danger (e.g., in the open with many enemies in [[line-of-sight|Line of Sight (LoS)]]), the Task Group may:
- **Ignore its Order** and Pull Back all FEs (see [[assault#Pull Back|Pull Back]])
- On the **next Turn**, they must receive New Orders

> [!warning]
> This rule does **not** apply to [[russia|Russia]] in Advanced mode ([[red-doctrine|Red Doctrine]]).

## New Orders

Issued during the [[command-phase|Command Phase]] (step 2.2), New Orders replace existing Orders/Fire Plans on the [[sync-matrix|Sync Matrix]] for any number of remaining turns.

**Process:**
1. Strike through existing Orders/Fire Plans on the Sync Matrix
2. Write down the New Orders
3. Place a **Reorg marker** on all [[force-elements|FEs]] in the Task Group / [[support-assets|Support Asset (SA)]]
4. Flip the [[task-groups|Task Group (TGp)]] marker to its striped side
5. The Task Group / [[support-assets|SA]] **cannot be activated** this turn
6. Opponent draws **1 [[electronic-warfare|Electronic Transmission (ETX)]]** per Task Group/[[support-assets|SA]] receiving New Orders

> [!note] Design note
> Issuing New Orders is costly — it loses a turn and generates [[electronic-warfare|ETX]]. But without New Orders, the original plan must be followed even if it no longer makes sense.

### Mission Command (Advanced, NATO Only)

See [[mission-command]]. NATO may issue New Orders to **one Task Group per turn** without:
- Placing Reorg markers
- Generating [[electronic-warfare|ETX]]
- Losing the activation

### Red Doctrine (Advanced, Russia)

See [[red-doctrine]]. Russia **cannot issue New Orders** at all. The pre-game Sync Matrix must be followed rigidly. If an Order cannot be fulfilled, Russia must attempt it on subsequent turns until a new pre-written Order takes effect.

## Order Verbs

Taken from Player Aid 6 (Orders Table). The verbs from the UK Staff Officers' Handbook include actions like:
- **Seize** — take and hold an objective
- **Screen** — observe and report
- **Secure** — protect an area
- **Withdraw** — move away from the enemy
- **Manoeuvre** — move to a position
- And others (full list on Player Aid 6)

Players can use **alternative Order Verbs** if they prefer — the important thing is clarity of intent.
