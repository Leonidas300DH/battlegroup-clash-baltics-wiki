# Initiative

Initiative determines **which side gets to choose who activates first** in each turn's [[execution-phase#Action Round|Action Round]]. It is critically important — the Initiative side controls the tempo of the turn.

## Determining Initiative

At the start of the [[execution-phase|Execution Phase]]:

1. Both sides roll **1D6**
2. Apply modifiers from the **Initiative Table** (Player Aid 1)
3. Highest modified roll wins Initiative for the turn

### Tie-breaking
- If tied: the side whose [[force-elements|Headquarters (HQ)]] has the **higher [[glossary|Command & Control (C2)]] Rating** wins
- If still tied: both sides roll again

## Turn 1

The scenario specifies which side has Initiative on Turn 1 — it is **not rolled**.

## What Initiative Does

The Initiative side decides **which side activates first**. This means:
- They can choose to go first (to strike before the opponent can react)
- They can choose to let the opponent go first (to see what the enemy does before committing)

> [!tip]
> From the [[rulebook-final|example of play]]: Russia had Initiative in Turn 1 and chose to go first, activating the [[support-assets|Unmanned Aerial Support drone (UAS)]]. NATO was deliberately waiting to see where Russian forces would appear, using Overwatch Actions for all Task Groups.

## HQ C2 Rating Impact

[[force-elements|HQ]] [[force-elements|Force Elements (FEs)]]' **[[glossary|C2]] Rating** provides a [[glossary|Dice Roll Modifier (DRM)]] for the Initiative roll, making it a valuable stat beyond just [[command-activation|Command Activations]] and [[morale#Rally Tests|Rally Tests]].

## Strategic Significance

Winning Initiative allows:
- **Offensive side**: strike before the defender can set up Overwatch
- **Defensive side**: place Overwatch markers before the attacker moves
- **React to information**: see what the opponent did before committing your main forces
- **Tempo control**: force the opponent to react to your actions
