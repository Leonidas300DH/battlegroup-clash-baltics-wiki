# Red Doctrine (Advanced Mode — Russia Only)

Red Doctrine reflects [[russia|Russia's]] doctrinal rigidity. Once the [[sync-matrix|Sync Matrix]] is written, Russian forces **cannot deviate** from it in any way.

## Restrictions

In Advanced mode, Russian [[task-groups|Task Groups]] and [[support-assets|Support Assets (SAs)]]:
- **Cannot issue [[orders#New Orders|New Orders]]** — the Sync Matrix is final
- **Cannot change [[fire-plans|Fire Plans]]**
- **Cannot target enemies** with [[indirect-fire|Indirect Fire]] outside the Fire Plan area
- **Cannot use "Moving to Safety"** — the option to ignore Orders and Pull Back does not apply
- **Must always follow Orders** — the "Adherence to Orders" flexibility does not apply

If a Russian Task Group **cannot fulfil** an Order due to delay or losses, it should attempt to fulfil it on following turns, or until a turn when a new pre-written Order takes effect.

> [!warning]
> This means the entire Russian plan must be written **perfectly** during the Preparation Phase. There are no second chances. Mistakes in the Sync Matrix are permanent.

## Compensating Advantages

To balance this rigidity, Russia receives:
- **More Fire Missions** — additional 152mm Artillery and [[support-assets|First Person View drone (FPV)]] Fire Missions in Advanced mode [[glossary|Order of Battle (ORBAT)]]s
- **[[electronic-warfare|Electronic Warfare (EW)]] superiority** — draws **2 [[electronic-warfare|EW Chits]]**, keeps 1 (chosen before NATO draws) in the [[electronic-warfare|EW]] Activity step
- Russia cannot be forced to Reassign FEs between Task Groups under the [[optional-rules#Flexible Task Groups|Flexible Task Groups]] optional rule

> [!note] Design note
> Russian doctrine typically requires a developed plan to be followed rigidly. This is offset by Russia's emphasis on long-range fires and Electronic Warfare, reflected in the additional Fire Missions and EW advantage.

## Strategic Implications

- **Plan for contingencies** — write Orders that account for possible delays and enemy positions
- **Fire Plans are everything** — since they can't be changed, choose target areas that will remain relevant across multiple turns
- **Accept losses** — Russian Task Groups cannot retreat from a bad situation; they must press on
- **Leverage fires** — use your superior artillery and [[support-assets|FPV]] to shape the battlefield before ground forces arrive
