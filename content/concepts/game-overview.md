# Game Overview

**Battlegroup Clash: Baltics (BC:B)** is a two- to six-player tactical combat game simulating hypothetical near-future land battles in **Estonia** between [[nato|NATO]] and [[russia|Russia]].

## Scope & Scale

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Level | Company-level |
| Unit size | [[force-elements|Platoon (PL)]] and [[force-elements|Section (SECT)]] |
| Largest scenario | NATO Battlegroup vs Russian Motorised Rifle Battalion |
| Map scale | 1 cm = 100 m |
| Grid Squares | 1,000 m x 1,000 m |
| Turn duration | 15-30 minutes real-time |
| Turns per scenario | 5 or 6 |
| Scenarios | 6 included |

## Design Origins

BC:B is based on **Battlegroup Wargame System (BGWS)**, a professional wargame designed by Ivor Gardiner of Vedette Consulting for the **British Army**. Game designer James Buckley adapted BGWS for commercial release by:

- Replacing Ordnance Survey maps with bespoke computer-aided design maps based on Estonian satellite imagery
- Moving Force Element stats onto counters (BGWS uses separate sheets)
- Removing umpire-dependent and military-judgement-dependent mechanics
- Replacing British-specific equipment (81mm mortars → 120mm mortars common across NATO)

## What Players Control

Players control:
- **[[force-elements|Force Elements (FEs)]]** — armour, infantry, reconnaissance, [[force-elements|Headquarters (HQ)]], A1 Echelon (supply trucks)
- **[[support-assets|Support Assets (SAs)]]** — artillery (155mm/152mm), [[support-assets|First Person View drone (FPV)]] drones, [[support-assets|Unmanned Aerial Support drone (UAS)]] reconnaissance drones
- **[[task-groups|Task Groups]]** — organizational groupings of [[force-elements|FEs]] that activate together

## Core Design Principles

1. **Planning matters** — the [[sync-matrix|Sync Matrix]] requires players to plan their entire game before Turn 1
2. **Combined arms** — rewards integrating infantry, armour, artillery, drones, and reconnaissance
3. **Counters as approximation** — positions are approximate, not exact; "a bit of judgement" is expected
4. **No concealment** — ubiquitous drone coverage means nothing stays hidden
5. **No air support** — contested airspace in the Baltics means no fixed-wing or rotary aviation
6. **Scarcity** — forces are below strength, ammo is limited, reflecting real-world constraints
7. **Electronic warfare** — radio emissions create vulnerabilities; managing your electronic signature is critical

## Player Count

- **Solo**: play both sides to best of ability
- **2 players**: one per side (most common)
- **Teams (2-3 per side)**: split control of [[task-groups|Task Groups]], optionally with an overall Commander directing Orders and activation priorities

## Dice

BC:B uses **six-sided dice (D6)**:
- **Red D6** and **Blue D6**: used for tests and combat resolution (roll 1D6 or 2D6)
- **Green D6**: Turn Tracker Die
- **[[glossary|Dice Roll Modifiers (DRMs)]]**: positive or negative numbers applied to modify die results

## Victory

Each scenario has a **Commander's Intent** with:
- **Primary Objective** — fixed
- **Secondary Objective** — secretly rolled before the game

Victory levels: **Decisive > Substantive > Marginal > Fail**. Both sides can achieve a victory (but not both Decisive).
