# Glossary & Acronyms

Every acronym and key term used in BC:B, with plain-English definitions and links to the relevant wiki pages.

---

## A

**A1 Echelon** — Logistical unit (trucks or unmanned vehicles) used to transport [[ammo|Ammo]] and [[atgm|Anti-Tank Guided Missiles (ATGMs)]] for resupply. Called "Sect" on the Russian side. Only used in Advanced mode. See [[force-elements#A1 Echelons]].

**Apers** — **Anti-personnel**. A type of Firepower (green box on the counter) used to target Foot (infantry) [[force-elements|Force Elements]]. See [[direct-fire#Firepower and Targeting]].

**ATGM** — **Anti-Tank Guided Missile**. A weapon marker (NLAW, Javelin, RPG, Stabber) that gives a [[force-elements|Force Element (FE)]] a gold Anti-tank capability. See [[atgm]].

**Atk** — **Anti-tank**. A type of Firepower (purple or gold box on the counter) used to target vehicle FEs. See [[direct-fire#Firepower and Targeting]].

---

## B

**BC:B** — **Battlegroup Clash: Baltics**. The full name of this game. See [[game-overview]].

**BGWS** — **Battlegroup Wargame System**. The professional wargame designed by Vedette Consulting for the British Army, on which BC:B is based. See [[game-overview#Design Origins]].

---

## C

**C2** — **Command & Control**. Refers to the ability to direct forces. In BC:B, the C2 Rating on [[force-elements#HQs|Headquarters (HQ) counters]] provides [[glossary|Dice Roll Modifiers (DRMs)]] for [[initiative|Initiative]], [[assault|Assaults]], [[morale#Rally Tests|Rally tests]], and enables [[command-activation|Command Activations]].

**CE** — **Combat Effectiveness**. The ability of a [[force-elements|FE]] to continue fighting, determined by its [[combat-effectiveness|Resilience]] and [[morale|Morale Status]].

---

## D

**D** — **Disrupted**. The second-worst [[morale|Morale Status]]. Reduces [[force-elements|Troop Quality (TQ)]] by -2, halves movement, prevents moving toward visible enemies, and prevents [[assault|Assaulting]]. One step from Broken (Elimination). See [[morale#Morale Status Levels]].

**D6** — **Six-sided die**. BC:B uses 2D6 (two dice, results added together) for most tests. A 1D6 roll uses a single die. See [[game-overview#Dice]].

**DRM** — **Dice Roll Modifier**. A positive or negative number applied to a die roll to reflect favorable or unfavorable conditions. Example: firing at a target in Cover applies a negative DRM.

---

## E

**E** — **Easting**. The vertical Grid Lines on the map. Numbers increase as you move east. Part of the map coordinate system. See [[terrain#Maps]].

**ETX** — **Electronic Transmission**. Markers representing radio/electronic emissions generated by certain actions. The opponent draws face-down [[electronic-warfare|ETX]] markers, which later become [[electronic-warfare#Target Markers|Target markers]] and trigger [[electronic-warfare#EW Chits|Electronic Warfare (EW) Chit]] draws. See [[electronic-warfare]].

**EW** — **Electronic Warfare**. The system of [[electronic-warfare|ETX]] markers, Target markers, and [[electronic-warfare|EW]] Chits that simulates electronic surveillance, jamming, and countermeasures. A core mechanic of BC:B. See [[electronic-warfare]].

---

## F

**F** — **Foot**. A [[force-elements|Force Element (FE)]] type representing infantry. Identified by a green diamond on the counter. Can enter Dense Wood, dig [[dig-shell-scrape|Shell Scrapes]], and is required for [[assault|Assaults]]. See [[force-elements#FE Types]].

**FE** — **Force Element**. Any military unit in the game represented by a counter on the map. Can be Foot, Wheeled, or Tracked; [[force-elements|Platoon (PL)]] or [[force-elements|Section (SECT)]] sized. See [[force-elements]].

**FPV** — **First Person View Drone**. A [[support-assets|Support Asset (SA)]] representing any drone with offensive capability. Fires [[indirect-fire|Indirect Fire]] from off-map, can [[indirect-fire#FPV Double-Tap|Double-Tap]] the same target. See [[support-assets#FPV]].

**FST** — **Fire Support Team**. A [[force-elements|Section (SECT)]]-sized [[force-elements|FE]] that serves as an [[force-elements#ISTAR Assets|Intelligence, Surveillance, Target Acquisition & Reconnaissance (ISTAR) Asset]], providing [[line-of-sight|Line of Sight (LoS)]] for [[indirect-fire|Indirect Fire]].

---

## G

**GI** — **Grid Intersection**. The precise point where an Easting and Northing Grid Line cross. Used for [[fire-plans|Fire Plans]] and [[support-assets|Unmanned Aerial Support drone (UAS)]] positioning. See [[terrain#Maps]].

**GL** — **Grid Line**. The numbered lines on the map (vertical = Easting, horizontal = Northing), spaced 10cm apart. See [[terrain#Maps]].

**GS** — **Grid Square**. A 10cm × 10cm square on the map (1,000m × 1,000m in game terms). Identified by Easting + Northing (e.g., GS E32/N49). See [[terrain#Maps]].

---

## H

**HQ** — **Headquarters**. A special [[force-elements|FE]] type (Main or Tac) with a [[command-activation|Command & Control (C2) Rating]]. Provides [[glossary|DRMs]], Rally test bonuses, and Command Activations. Each side has two (Main + Tac). See [[force-elements#HQs]].

---

## I

**ID** — **Identification**. The text on an FE counter identifying which unit it represents (e.g., "1-A-1" = 1st Platoon, A Company, 1st Battalion).

**ISTAR** — **Intelligence, Surveillance, Target Acquisition, and Reconnaissance**. Marked by a star (★) on counters. ISTAR Assets (Recce, FST, [[force-elements|HQ]], [[support-assets|UAS]]) provide [[line-of-sight|LoS]] for [[indirect-fire|Indirect Fire]]. See [[force-elements#ISTAR Assets]].

---

## L

**LOC** — **Line of Communication**. Supply and communication routes. Referenced in scenario briefings (e.g., capturing a bridge for an additional LOC).

**LoS** — **Line of Sight**. The ability to visually detect a target. Drawn from any part of the sighting FE's counter to any part of the target's counter. Maximum 3,000m (4,000m for Recce). Blocked by Obstructions. See [[line-of-sight]].

---

## N

**N** — **Northing**. The horizontal Grid Lines on the map. Numbers increase as you move north. See [[terrain#Maps]].

---

## O

**ORBAT** — **Order of Battle**. The list of [[force-elements|FEs]] and [[support-assets|Support Assets (SAs)]] each side has in a scenario, including their Task Group assignments, [[atgm|ATGMs]], starting Ammo, and other details. Found in the Scenario Booklets.

---

## P

**PL** — **Platoon**. The larger [[force-elements|FE]] size (3 dots on counter). Typically 25-30 soldiers or 3-4 vehicles. Has Resilience of 3-4 and uses two physical counters. See [[force-elements#FE Sizes]].

---

## R

**RECCE** — **Reconnaissance**. A [[force-elements|Section (SECT)]]-sized [[force-elements|FE]] specializing in observation. Has 4,000m [[line-of-sight|LoS]] range (vs standard 3,000m), 500m [[force-elements|ISTAR]] exception in Dense Woods/Urban, and reduced Artillery [[indirect-fire#Danger Close|Danger Close]] (250m instead of 500m). An [[force-elements|ISTAR]] Asset.

**REPLENDEM** — **Replenishment Demand**. An Advanced mode Action where an A1 Echelon FE resupplies [[ammo|Ammo]] and [[atgm|ATGMs]] to friendly FEs within 250m. See [[replendem]].

**RPG** — **Rocket Propelled Grenade**. A short-range [[atgm|Anti-Tank Guided Missile (ATGM)]] used by [[russia|Russian]] Foot [[force-elements|FEs]]. Classified as an [[atgm|ATGM]] in BC:B.

---

## S

**S** — **Suppressed**. A [[morale|Morale Status]] level. Reduces [[force-elements|TQ]] by -1 and halves movement. See [[morale#Morale Status Levels]].

**SA** — **Support Asset**. Off-map assets representing artillery and drones (155mm/152mm Artillery, [[support-assets|First Person View drone (FPV)]], [[support-assets|Unmanned Aerial Support drone (UAS)]]). Cannot be targeted. See [[support-assets]].

**SECT** — **Section**. The smaller [[force-elements|FE]] size (1 dot on counter). Typically 8-10 soldiers or 1-2 vehicles. Has Resilience of 1-2. Can [[stacking|Embed]] with a Tracked [[force-elements|PL]]. See [[force-elements#FE Sizes]].

---

## T

**T** — **Tracked**. A [[force-elements|FE]] type representing armoured vehicles ([[force-elements|Armoured Personnel Carriers (APCs)]], [[force-elements|Infantry Fighting Vehicles (IFVs)]], [[force-elements|Main Battle Tanks (MBTs)]]). Identified by a purple or gold square on the counter. Purple = [[force-elements|APC]]/[[force-elements|IFV]], gold = [[force-elements|MBT]]. See [[force-elements#FE Types]].

**Tac HQ** — **Tactical Headquarters**. One of two HQ types. Limited range for [[command-activation|Command Activations]] (1,000m). See [[force-elements#HQs]].

**TGp** — **Task Group**. A group of [[force-elements|FEs]] organised together to meet specific objectives. The unit of activation in BC:B. See [[task-groups]].

**TQ** — **Troop Quality**. A number (3-6) on the [[force-elements|FE]] counter representing training, cohesion, and will to fight. Provides [[glossary|DRMs]] for [[direct-fire|Direct Fire]], [[assault|Assaults]], and [[morale#Rally Tests|Rally tests]]. Reduced by [[morale|Suppression/Disruption]] and by losing Resilience. See [[force-elements#FE Stats]].

---

## U

**UAS** — **Unmanned Aerial Support Drone**. A [[support-assets|Support Asset (SA)]] placed on the map at Grid Intersections. Provides [[force-elements|ISTAR]] [[line-of-sight|LoS]] to the 4 adjacent Grid Squares, ignoring all Obstructions. Cannot fire. See [[support-assets#UAS]].

---

## W

**W** — **Wheeled**. A [[force-elements|FE]] type representing trucks and motorbikes. Identified by a grey circle on the counter. Targetable by any Firepower type. See [[force-elements#FE Types]].
